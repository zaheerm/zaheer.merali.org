## Tone & Voice

### Do
- **Direct and experience-backed.** Lead with what you've built, not what you've read. "I shipped this" > "I think this."
- **Technically confident but accessible.** You can explain complex systems to a broad audience — your recommendations praise your patience and nuance.
- **Conversational, not corporate.** Your best-performing post opened with "My train, coach or bus journeys now:" — casual, relatable, no jargon.
- **Show vulnerability.** The hospital setback before the half marathon resonated because it was real. Don't polish away the struggle.
- **First-person storytelling.** "I" statements with concrete details beat abstract advice.

### Don't
- Don't write like a press release. No "thrilled to announce" or "excited to share" openers (you've used these — they underperform compared to your casual posts).
- Don't over-hashtag. One or two targeted hashtags max.
- Don't repost without adding your own perspective.
- Don't write walls of text. Your best posts are 3-5 short paragraphs.

### Signature Moves
- **The practitioner's hot take:** Bold opinion backed by real experience. "The whole landscape has changed for software more so by AI than any other one thing that I've experienced in my career."
- **The behind-the-scenes moment:** Show the messy reality of building at scale, not the polished version.
- **The crossover:** Connect endurance sports to engineering lessons. The discipline transfers.

## Visibility Hacks

### 1. Leverage Your Speaking Credentials
You've spoken at Nvidia GTC and Cloud Native London. These aren't easy stages to get on.

- **Before the talk:** Post about what you'll cover and why it matters. Tag the conference.
- **During:** Share a photo or key slide with a single insight.
- **After:** Write a "3 things I learned" post. This triples the content from one event.
- **Apply to speak more:** KubeCon (you've attended), QCon London, AI Engineer Summit, Cisco Live. Your Nvidia GTC credential opens doors.

### 2. Turn Your Best Posts Into Series
Your AI coding on the phone post hit 2,600+ impressions with zero media. Imagine a weekly "AI Developer Diary" series:

- **Week 1:** Reviewing a PR from a train with Claude Code
- **Week 2:** How I use AI to architect a new service
- **Week 3:** What AI still can't do — where human judgment matters
- **Format:** Short text post + one screenshot or photo. Consistency beats virality.

### 3. The Webex Covid Scaling Story
This is your untold blockbuster. "Orders of magnitude scaling in weeks" during Covid is the kind of story that gets thousands of impressions. Write it as a detailed narrative post or a short article. Tag it for the distributed systems and SRE communities.

### 4. Comment Strategically
Your 1,609 followers is a solid base but can grow faster through comments.

- Comment on posts from: AI leaders at Cisco, AWS, Nvidia. Cloud-native community figures. GStreamer/open source maintainers.
- Add substance, not "Great post!" Share a relevant experience or a contrarian angle.
- Spend 10 minutes a day commenting before you post. The algorithm rewards active users.

### 5. Cross-Pollinate Audiences
You have distinct audiences that don't overlap:
- **Tech/AI** people from your engineering content
- **Cycling/endurance** community from DesiRidersUK and running
- **Open source** community from GStreamer

Posts that bridge two worlds perform best. "What 550km of cycling taught me about scaling distributed systems" would outperform either topic alone.

### 6. Optimise Posting Cadence
Based on your analytics (2,600+ impressions on your best recent post with 1,609 followers):

- **Target:** 2-3 posts per week (you currently post ~1/month — this is the biggest lever)
- **Best times for UK/US overlap:** Tuesday-Thursday, 8-9am GMT (catches US East Coast morning)
- **Mix:** 2 text posts + 1 media post (photo, video, or carousel) per week
- **Engage in comments for 15-20 min after posting** to boost early distribution

### 7. Pin Your Best Content
Update your Featured section with:
1. The Nvidia GTC panel (establishes AI credibility)
2. The Basrah2Karbala video (shows the whole person)
3. A future "scaling Webex during Covid" article

### 8. Update Your Headline
Current: "Principal Software Engineer (Director level) at Cisco Collab AI"

Consider: "Architecting Enterprise AI at Cisco | 25 Years in Open Source | Scaling Webex Through a Pandemic"

This tells a story instead of just listing a title. It gives people three reasons to follow you.
